Hi,

I am a Psychology PhD Student at the University of Cambridge, and a Visiting Fellow within the Organisational Behaviour Program at Harvard University.

Being trained as a Psychologist as well as Economist I try to combine both perspectives in my daily work. I hold a master’s degree in Psychology (University of Cambridge, UK) and bachelor’s degree in Psychology and Economics (Clark University, USA). Previously I worked at the Economics Department at Yale, and at the Psychology Departments at Harvard, Cornell, Clark and the Weizmann Institute of Science (Israel).

I am interested in understanding behaviour through an interactionalist lens, combining geo-tagged personality and environmental information. One of my latest projects used, e.g. Yelp data in combination with a large scale personality survey to link neighbourhood personalities and local amenities. In general, I am interested in spatial (econometric) analysis, our NBER working paper allows to control for spatial change in a repeated cross sectional design, and it is used, e.g. by the Joint Economic Committee of the United States Congress.

I also work as Lab Manager for the Cambridge Personality & Social Dynamics Group, feel free to check out our work and message me if you are interested in joining our group as a Summer/Visiting Research Assistant!

Interests
